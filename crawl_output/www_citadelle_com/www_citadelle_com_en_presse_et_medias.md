Annual press kit 
2025
The history of the Besançon Citadel is closely linked to that of its architect, Vauban. Vauban, who drew up the plans for the building in 1668, left a deep imprint on the whole city. In the heart of the city, no fewer than 18 other Vauban heritage sites punctuate the fortifications, reinforcing the astonishing heritage identity of the Comtois capital. This first-rate military architectural ensemble has been on UNESCO's World Heritage List since July 2008. With an average of 290,000 visitors a year, and a wide range of attractions (heritage, history, museums, biodiversity, cultural programs, etc.), the Besançon Citadel is a must-see site in the Bourgogne-Franche-Comté region.

Vauban monument
A royal season at La Citadelle! 
17/04/2025 
In 1675, work began on the Citadelle de Besançon, designed by Vauban on the orders of Louis XIV to reinforce the kingdom's defences. Three and a half centuries later, this fortress, a UNESCO World Heritage Site, remains a remarkable testimony to 17th-century military engineering and a symbol of the strategic genius of its era. To mark this exceptional anniversary, the Citadelle will be transformed into a veritable living theater throughout the year. A rich and immersive program of exhibitions, workshops, themed tours and events plunges you into the world of the Sun King.

Museum of Resistance and Deportation
Press kit on the Besançon Museum of the Resistance and Deportation 
22/05/2023 
Open since 1971, the Musée de la Résistance et de la Déportation de Besançon (MRDB) is a benchmark among museums dealing with the Second World War. It is located in the heart of a heritage complex, the Citadelle, and in the immediate vicinity of a place of remembrance, the Fusillés monument. Thanks to its location within the Citadelle, the museum benefits from the strong appeal of Franche-Comté's leading tourist site, with some 270,000 visitors a year, and is one of the must-visit sites in the Bourgogne-Franche-Comté region (4th most visited paying site after the Hospices de Beaune, the Parc des Combes and the Guédelon medieval construction site).

Annual press kit 
14/03/2023 
The history of the Besançon Citadel is closely linked to that of its architect: Sébastien Le Prestre, better known as Vauban. Engineer, architect, town planner, hydraulic engineer, economist, philosopher and strategist, this jack-of-all-trades first drew up plans for the citadel in 1668. Often compared to Leonardo da Vinci, the Marquis de Vauban not only left his mark on the whole of the Comtoise capital, but also gave Besançon a first-rate architectural ensemble, now a UNESCO World Heritage Site open to the public.

Museum of Resistance and Deportation
La Citadelle de Besançon: Launch of work on the Besançon Museum of the Resistance and Deportation 
01/06/2022 
Since 1971, the Musée de la Résistance et de la Déportation has been a benchmark among sites dedicated to the Second World War. Since January 2020, the museum has undergone a 360-degree metamorphosis: completely redesigned spaces, original museography, personification of history through personal life stories, enhancement of collections, dynamic cultural programming and, last but not least, a strong commitment to research.
