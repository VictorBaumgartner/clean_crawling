## Rules of procedure
Having regard to the Code Général des Collectivités Territoriales and in particular articles R.2221-28 and R.2221-29,
Considering the Citadelle complex, the three museums of France including the museum and its zoological garden, its aquarium, its insectarium, its noctarium and all the facilities made available to the public, in accordance with law no. 76.629 du 10 juillet 1976 relative à la protection de la nature et de l'Arrêté du 25 mars 2004 fixant les règles générales de fonctionnement et les caractéristiques générales des installations des établissements zoologiques à caractère fixe et permanent, présentant au public des spécimens vivants de la faune locale ou étrangère et relevant de la rubrique 21-40 de la nomenclature des installations classées pour la protection de l'environnement,
In view of circular no. 99-136 of September 21, 1999 on the organization of school outings in public nursery and elementary schools
In view of the Certificate of Competence provided for in the Order of March 25, 2004, which stipulates that the aforementioned installations are under the responsibility of the Certificate holders,
Considering that it is in everyone's interest to set out the terms and conditions for the operation, use and access of the entire site,
### Article 1: Scope of application
This decree applies to the entire Citadelle, its museums, including the zoological garden, aquarium, insectarium and noctarium, and to all facilities made available to the public.
### Article 2: Access conditions
The part of the site between the Front Saint-Etienne and the Front Royal is freely accessible, apart from the fenced-off prohibited areas and security zones.
From the Front Royal onwards, and to visit the museums in particular, visitors must pay an admission fee, the rates of which are displayed at the ticket office.The ticket entitles the holder to access the site for the day on which the ticket is purchased, regardless of the number of times the visitor enters or leaves.
The public has no access to areas under construction or to service areas.
Minors under the age of 16 must be accompanied by a legal guardian or an adult with the guardian's authorization to be admitted to the site.
Minors over the age of 16 must either be accompanied in the same way, or have authorization from their legal representatives. The site reserves the right to check the age and existence of authorizations.
School groups must be supervised in accordance with circular no. 99-136 of September 21, 1999 on the organization of school outings in public nursery and elementary schools. La Citadelle staff are not present to take the place of group leaders. In the event of an incident, the responsibility of the group leaders will be sought first.
Excluding school groups, there must be one responsible adult for every 8 children under 6, and one responsible adult for every 12 children over 6.
For groups made up of disabled people, one guide is required for every 6 disabled people.
### Article 3: Opening hours
#### La Citadelle is open to the public according to the season:
 * from 10 a.m. to 5 p.m.
 * 9 a.m. to 6 p.m.
 * 9 a.m. to 7 p.m.

Visitors are invited to refer to the opening times displayed at the entrance to the site or on the website: www.citadelle.com.The museums, including the animal areas, close at least a quarter of an hour before the site closes.In the event of bad weather or for operational reasons, these opening times may be modified.
For the same reasons, the site may be temporarily closed to the public and evacuated in whole or in part.
### Article 4: Prohibitions
#### It is strictly forbidden to :
 * activities likely to disturb the peaceful enjoyment of the site
 * endanger the peace and safety of the public, staff and animals on display
 * cause damage to plantings, structures, museum collections or buildings
 * generate various types of pollution
 * unless otherwise authorized, enter the site by vehicle
 * cross fences and safety zones
 * damage or deteriorate buildings, fences, signs, etc.
 * throw anything into the well
 * to climb into the wheel next to the well
 * leave visiting aisles, climb walls, enter service areas
 * bring in foreign animals (dogs, cats, even on a leash, as well as NAC), with the exception of dogs for the blind and police dogs. Under no circumstances may the latter enter the small farm.
 * Bring in sound instruments (radios or other), rolling objects (bicycles, scooters, skates, skateboards, skis, sledges, etc.), except strollers and wheelchairs for disabled persons, and flying objects (e.g. kites, drones, etc.).
 * bring in weapons (firearms, knives, cutters, scissors, etc.) and any sharp, perforating or blunt objects, as well as dangerous products (explosive materials, aerosols, lasers, etc.).
 * introduce illegal substances
 * consume alcohol on the site outside of events and receptions authorized and supervised by the owner
 * block access to the stairs leading to the ramparts
 * fires and barbecues
 * throw stones or any other object
 * move benches and litter garbage cans
 * knocking on windows or fences, exciting animals, throwing objects at them
 * feed the animals,
 * attract or touch animals, except pets at the Petite Ferme (in compliance with the instructions given by our staff)
 * smoking and vaping in the buildings and zoo (zoo garden and aquarium courtyard)
 * eating or drinking in museums or temporary exhibition halls
 * practice any individual or group sport, including high-risk activities (climbing, paragliding, base-jumping, etc.), except under special agreement with Citadelle management
 * walk barefoot in the fee-paying part of the site and in the buildings
 * wandering around drunk or behaving in an aggressive or dangerous manner towards staff, other visitors or animals, and more generally, endangering oneself or others.

### Article 5: Visitor's obligations
 * Respect the site and staff.
 * Respect animals, plantations, collections, buildings, places of remembrance and commemoration (e.g.: space for the posts of those who were shot).
 * Comply with the instructions, remarks and injunctions of site personnel.
 * Constant and effective supervision of children.
 * Respect all prohibition, information and warning signs.
 * Use caution on sloping aisles, and avoid stairways with baby carriages or wheelchairs for the disabled.

### Article 6: Special instructions
 * Visitors are expected to dress appropriately and show respect for the premises and other visitors.
 * Visitors may be asked to open their bags at the request of staff for a visual check.
 * For the peace and quiet of authorized animals, please do not run or shout.
 * Unless otherwise indicated at the entrance to a space, photographs and films (without flash or additional lighting) are authorized for private use only. Professionals, on the other hand, are required to obtain prior written consent from the management.
 * Gatherings on the site are not permitted, except for visits, events, meetings, receptions or commemorations scheduled and approved by management.
 * Individual or family picnics are permitted on the lawns and on the furniture made available to the public for this purpose. They are not permitted in the restaurant areas or on their terraces. Cleanliness and peace and quiet must be respected.

### Article 7: Liability
Visitors are responsible for damage of any kind caused by themselves, persons, animals or objects in their care or custody.
The City of Besançon declines all responsibility for accidents caused by failure to comply with the present regulations or with basic safety rules.
In the event of an accident, the City of Besançon will take all necessary steps to determine the cause of the accident and the responsibilities involved.
### Article 8: Publication and adherence to the rules
These rules and regulations can be consulted at any of the site's ticket offices and on our website: www.citadelle.com.The mere fact of entering the Citadelle implies full acceptance of these rules and regulations.
### Article 9: Penalties
Any infringement of these rules may result in the exclusion of the offender(s) from the site , without refund of entry fees.
Anyone behaving aggressively or dangerously towards staff, other visitors or animals, or putting themselves or others in danger, may be expelled by the police.
